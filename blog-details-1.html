ASSISTIVE TECHNOLOGY AND ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN HEALTHCARE.

  • HOME
  • WEBINARS
  • How Assistive technology [AT] powered by Artificial intelligence [AI] impacts healthcare?
DECEMBER 30, 2020

Assistive technology is any product or software program that helps a person with a disability to lead a normal life in many aspects and AI is simulating machines to think like a human. Assistive technology powered by artificial intelligence [AI] has helped to overcome the main challenges faced by a person with a disability. AT helps people with disabilities to live life independently or with less dependence.



Assistive technology improves the quality of lives of people with difficulty in speaking, writing, remembering, walking, seeing, hearing each requiring its assistive technologies.AT devices like mobility aids, hearing aids, cognitive aids for lack of thinking skills, automatic page- turners, book holder, adaptive book holder to improve educational skills are available

The greatest of all applications of AI in AT is that of a robot that helps disabled people especially those living alone. These home robots can be customized based on disability and can make emergency calls during a medical situation to keeping track of medications and dates.

The advantage of using AI in healthcare is the ability to process data that it collects periodically from the real world and converts it into useful output.AI makes the process of tracking data very easy and effective. A wide range of datasets collected by AI can perform clinical diagnosis and it can even predict results.AI is used for decision making in each phase of diagnosis and suggests subsequent treatment.

AI has made diagnosing diseases cheaper and faster. Diagnosing a disease correctly requires years of expertise but AI has made it faster. Demand for expert doctors is high and supply is less which delays life-saving treatments. When there is plenty of good data available algorithms become as good as experts. AI derives conclusions within seconds from the wide range of data they have collected with more accuracy.
